Proto-Surf (Pre-1960)
---------------------

Johnny & The Hurricanes
        16 Greatest Hits
        Rock (??) CD 10009

Comments:  Johnny & the Hurricanes were OK, but I don't think they 
           played surf music.  There will always be borderline cases 
           in deciding who is "in" and who is "not in" a musical genre, 
           but I think it's generally accepted that to be considered 
           surf music a recording needs at least one of the following:  
           (1) reference to surfing or beaches in the title (or lyrics,
           for vocal surf music); (2) small combo (guitars-bass-drums, 
           possibly sax or electric piano) with melody lead played on 
           guitar; (3) reverb on lead or rhythm guitar; (4) recorded by 
           a Southern California band that was part of the original surf 
           culture 1960-65.  I don't think J&H qualify on any of these 
           scores.  Instrumental surf is not co-extensive with instrumental 
           rock and roll, but is a subcategory.

           (aek) Agreed.. The Neon Spores used to cover some of their stuff
           (on the other hand, they used to cover Billy Vaughn too :-) )
